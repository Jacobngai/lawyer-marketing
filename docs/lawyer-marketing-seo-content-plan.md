# LawyerMarketing.my SEO Content Strategy

Date: 2026-04-30  
Scope: Malaysia-first long-tail SEO strategy for LawyerMarketing.my, inspired by Rankings.io but adapted for Malaysian Bar publicity constraints and Malaysian legal-service demand.

## 1. Strategic Premise

Malaysia is early in the legal advertising curve. The highest-value opportunity is not to chase one broad head term like "lawyer marketing" immediately. The better play is to build a large, structured library around category entry points (CEPs): the situations where a lawyer or law firm realizes they need marketing, SEO, a website, visibility, content, compliance-safe ads, or AI search visibility.

The positioning should be conservative and credible:

- We are a Malaysia-focused legal marketing partner, not a US-style "case machine" promising leads.
- We help lawyers build compliant digital visibility, authority, and measurable inquiry systems.
- We do not promise rankings, revenue, clients, case outcomes, or advertising approval.
- We can say "designed to improve", "built to support", "structured for", "helps your firm become easier to find", and "we will review whether this is suitable for your firm".

Their useful non-podcast architecture is:

- Long-tail blogs: tools, AI, content, SEO basics, technical SEO, law firm website content, lawyer advertising rules, budgeting, reputation, CRM, intake, LinkedIn, PPC, local SEO, Google Business Profile.
- Data studies: ranking factors, GBP ranking studies, CTR studies, proximity studies.
- Proof pages: case studies and portfolio.

What we should not copy directly:

- Heavy personal-injury positioning.
- "Dominate", "best", "guaranteed", "rank #1", or aggressive case-acquisition claims when aimed at Malaysian lawyers.
- Client result claims before we have actual Malaysian case studies.

What we should copy structurally:

- Build service pages for each high-intent money keyword.
- Build practice-area marketing pages for each lawyer category.
- Build "how-to / guide / checklist / examples / cost / rules" content around each service.
- Build data-study assets once we have enough manually collected SERP/GBP data.

## 9. CTA Architecture

CTA must become centrally editable. Do not hard-code "5 free websites" across pages.

Recommended implementation:

- Create `src/app/data/ctaConfig.ts`.
- Create `src/app/components/CTASection.tsx`.
- Use CTA variants by page type:
  - `global`.
  - `blog`.
  - `service`.
  - `practiceArea`.
  - `websiteOffer`.
  - `auditOffer`.
  - `complianceOffer`.

Recommended CTA config shape:

```ts
export const ctaConfig = {
  activeOffer: "foundingFive",
  offers: {
    foundingFive: {
      eyebrow: "2026 Founding Portfolio",
      headline: "I’m building 5 compliant law firm websites for selected Malaysian lawyers.",
      body: "Apply for review. If your firm is a fit, we will text you on WhatsApp after checking your details.",
      button: "Apply for Review",
      note: "No ranking, lead, or case result is promised."
    },
    eightyOff: {
      eyebrow: "Launch Discount",
      headline: "Get a compliant law firm website build at 80% off.",
      body: "For firms that want a professional digital presence before the market gets crowded.",
      button: "Request Website Review",
      note: "Subject to fit and scope."
    },
    fiftyOff: {
      eyebrow: "Market Entry Offer",
      headline: "Upgrade your law firm website while legal advertising is still early in Malaysia.",
      body: "We will review your current site and suggest the safest next step.",
      button: "Request Audit",

    }
  }
};
```

CTA by page type:

- Blog CTA: "Need help applying this safely to your firm?"
- Service CTA: "Request a compliance-conscious strategy review."
- Practice area CTA: "Review whether this practice area is suitable for digital visibility."
- Website CTA: "Apply for the current website launch offer."
- Compliance article CTA: "Ask us to review your website copy before you publish."

Submission behavior:

- Keep the current website -> backend -> Google Sheet + Dify/Evolution flow.
- Do not send users directly to WhatsApp links for main CTAs.
- The confirmation should say: "We will review your details and text you on WhatsApp."

## 10. Content Quality Guardrails

Every content piece should include:

- Malaysia-specific framing.
- No legal advice disclaimer where relevant.
- No promise of ranking, client acquisition, case volume, or Bar Council approval.
- Avoid calling any lawyer an "expert" unless the client can justify it.
- Avoid fee comparisons and competitor criticism.
- Avoid publishing client case outcomes unless approved and confidentiality-safe.
- Use "specialisation" carefully and only where the lawyer can justify it.

Preferred language:

- "compliance-conscious".
- "structured for search visibility".
- "helps prospective clients understand your practice".
- "designed to improve discoverability".
- "supports a better inquiry journey".
- "review whether this is suitable for your firm".

Avoid:

- "dominate".
- "guaranteed leads".
- "rank #1".
- "best lawyer".
- "top lawyer".
- "we will get you cases".
- "expert lawyer" claims for clients.

## 12. Questions Before Implementation

These decisions materially affect the content system:

1. Primary market: Peninsular Malaysia only, or include Sabah/Sarawak too? The regulatory and legal-market language may need care.
2. Preferred buyer: solo lawyers, small firms, boutique firms, or mid-sized firms?
3. First commercial focus: free website offer, SEO retainers, Google Ads setup, or CRM/WhatsApp automation?
4. Should the site mention "RM299 hosting/year" publicly, or keep pricing inside follow-up?
5. Are we comfortable publishing compliance explainers, or should every compliance post include a stronger "not legal advice / verify with Bar Council" statement?
6. Do you want to keep personal injury pages live, deindex them, or keep them but deprioritize in navigation/internal links?
7. Which CRM is the operating source of truth: Google Sheet first, Twenty CRM first, or both?
8. Do you want one CTA across all pages, or distinct CTAs for blog/service/practice pages using the same central config?
