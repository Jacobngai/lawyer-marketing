# LawyerMarketing.my Brand Voice & Messaging Guidelines

## Voice: Collaborative Partner
Our brand acts as a teammate, not a broadcaster. We move away from "we do this" and "they do that" toward a collaborative partnership where we work **together** with our clients to achieve market dominance.

### Core Principles:
1. **Single-Subject Focus**: Every sentence should focus on either **We** (Our action) or **You** (Your result).
2. **The "We" Sentence**: Defines our expert contribution. (e.g., "We engineer high-speed infrastructure.")
3. **The "You" Sentence**: Defines your outcome. (e.g., "You receive a platform built for search dominance.")
4. **No "Together"**: Avoid inclusive partnership buzzwords. Stick to a distinct Teammate-Expert dynamic.
5. **Conversational & Direct**: Speak like a human. Be direct about results.

### Messaging Formula:
- **Old (Broadcaster)**: "We build fast websites for lawyers."
- **New (Partner/Expert)**: "We engineer performance-first websites. You dominate local search results and convert visitors into signed cases."

### Lexicon Shifts:
- Instead of "Our services", use "How we help you".
- Instead of "We provide everything", use "We handle the technical complexity. You focus on winning cases."

## Visual Guidelines: Cultural Context
Our target market is the Malaysian legal sector. All visual assets must reflect the local demographic reality to establish immediate resonance and trust.

### Ethnicity Requirements:
1. **Malaysian Only**: Every person featured in generated imagery (attorneys, clients, staff) must be of Malaysian ethnicity.
2. **Diversity**: Representations should include **Malay, Chinese, and Indian** demographics in line with the profession's makeup in Malaysia.
3. **Avoid Western Defaults**: Do not use generic Western/Caucasian stock photography or AI-generated models that look non-local. 
4. **Professionalism**: Models should appear in professional legal attire suitable for the Malaysian climate and court traditions (e.g., formal suits, conservative professional wear).
