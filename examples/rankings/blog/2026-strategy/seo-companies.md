Once you've narrowed down your list of potential SEO partners, take some time to ask them the right questions before signing a contract with them.
Having the skills and experience to work on your campaign is only a part of a good working relationship with an SEO agency.
When you speak with a possible SEO vendor, be sure to set budget expectations upfront. All the questions you could ask them will not matter if you and the vendor cannot agree on pricing.
You also have to ensure that their business style matches your own. Make sure you're on the same page regarding goals, expectations, reporting, and communication.

Here are the top questions that you should ask before finalizing a deal:
- What does a good partnership look like to you?
- What does success look like, and how is it measured?
- How often will you send reports or updates?
- What format will you report in?
- What metrics or KPIs will you report on?
- What services will we need, and which ones are not important?
- Do you have a background in my industry?
- Have you worked with my target audience before? If not, do you have a system for learning about and understanding them?
- Do you do customer research?
- What results have you achieved in the past with similar clients?
Asking these questions can help you determine if the potential partner has the experience necessary to drive the results you deserve and if your goals are in alignment.

### Who are the best SEO companies in 2026?
The best SEO companies in 2026 depend heavily on your industry and goals. Based on our research, Rankings.io leads for law firms, SimpleTiger for B2B SaaS, WebFX for local businesses, Siege Media for content-driven growth, and Orainti for international and technical SEO. The agencies that outperform in 2026 share one thing in common: genuine niche specialization combined with a clear strategy for AI-era search visibility.

### What does an SEO company do?
An SEO company improves your website's visibility in search engine results so more qualified people find your business organically, without paying for ads. In practice, this involves technical audits to fix site issues, keyword research to understand what your customers are searching for, on-page optimization to make your content more relevant, link building to increase your site's authority, and increasingly in 2026, Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) to ensure your brand appears in AI-powered search answers from tools like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google's AI Overviews.

### Is SEO dead or evolving in 2026?
SEO is not dead, but it is undergoing its most significant transformation since Google introduced mobile-first indexing. The December 2025 and March 2026 Google core updates specifically targeted thin, generic, and AI-generated content with no original perspective. At the same time, AI-powered search experiences are changing where and how people find answers. The agencies and websites winning in 2026 combine strong, traditional SEO fundamentals with content that demonstrates genuine first-hand expertise and structure it so AI engines can cite it. SEO is evolving faster than ever, but it is far from dead.

### How much should I expect to pay for SEO in 2026?
For most businesses, quality SEO services range from $2,500 to $10,000 per month. Local and small businesses can expect to pay on the lower end of that range, while competitive industries like legal, healthcare, and financial services typically require $5,000 to $15,000 or more per month to see meaningful results. Enterprise-level campaigns with national scope can exceed $100,000 per month. Be cautious of agencies offering SEO for $300 to $500 per month, at that price point, the work they do is either automated, outsourced at low quality, or simply insufficient to compete in any meaningful market.

### Can I do SEO by myself?
Yes, but with significant caveats. Basic on-page SEO: With training, a small business owner can manage optimizing page titles, writing clear meta descriptions, improving site speed, and publishing relevant content. However, competitive keyword research, technical SEO audits, link acquisition, and increasingly GEO optimization require specialist knowledge and significant time. If you operate in a competitive market or rely on organic search as a primary lead source, DIY SEO will almost always underperform compared to working with a dedicated professional SEO agency. The opportunity cost of doing it yourself typically exceeds the agency fee within the first year.

### Who is the best SEO company in the USA?
There is no single "best" SEO company in the USA. The right answer depends entirely on your industry, budget, and goals. That said, among the most consistently recognized agencies based on verified client reviews, proven results, and 2026 performance: WebFX leads for full-service and local SEO at scale, Rankings.io leads for law firms, SimpleTiger leads for B2B SaaS, and Siege Media leads for content-driven SEO programs. All four have documented track records and strong third-party ratings across Clutch and Google.

Google's AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity, and other AI-powered engines now answer many queries directly, which has reduced clicks to some informational pages. However, transactional and local searches, the queries that drive real business, still heavily depend on traditional and local SEO signals. The shift means that content that simply rephrases existing information is losing ground fast, while content built on original data, first-hand experience, and clear expertise is gaining visibility both in Google and in AI-generated answers. The best SEO strategy in 2026 combines traditional ranking signals with GEO, structuring your content so AI engines cite your brand as the authoritative answer.
