## 1. The Logic of Content Pruning

Content decays when it no longer meets the technical or professional standards of the current market. Outdated legal summaries, low-word-count "stubs," and irrelevant firm announcements dilute your authority. We categorize content into three logical actions:
- **Consolidate**: Merging overlapping articles into a single, comprehensive "Technical Pillar."
- **Optimize**: Updating high-potential pages with current statutes and 2026 AIO/GEO signals.
- **Prune**: Permanently redirecting or deleting low-value content that offers no conversion path.

## 2. Measuring Value Beyond Traffic

We prioritize **Acquisition Value** over raw vanity metrics. A page with low traffic but a high conversion rate for "Corporate Dispute Resolution" is a success. Your audit must blend traffic data with real-world intake metrics to determine the logical value of every URL in your structure.

## 3. Hardening Evidence & Authority (E-E-A-T)

In 2026, Google and AI assistants reward firms that demonstrate deep "Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness." An audit is the time to harden these signals—ensuring every piece of content is backed by technical precision and verifiable professional results.

## 4. Preparing for Generative Engines (GEO)

AI synthesizers require clear, structured, and factual content to cite your firm as an authority. During your audit, we restructure legacy content into the logical hierarchies that these engines prefer, ensuring your firm remains the "Preferred Citation" for jurisdictional search.
